White Space and Clean Design

Quip: Quip website

On this website, Quip uses active whitespace. Placing the toothbrush front and center surrounded by whitespace guides the user's eye directly to the subject and without question they makes clear what the company does. This, also serves as a guide to direct the user to the call-to-action buttons.

Visual Hierarchy

Simply Chocolate: Simply Chocolate website

Simply Chocolate, is a great example about how to use size and scale to emphasize visual hierarchy. The first thing users drawn to in the design is the huge text that explains what the product is all about in a way that doesn't detract from the other elements on the screen.

Rule of Thirds

Sia Aperitivos: Sia Aperitivos website

In this website the main idea about the Rule of Thirds is used is dividing the layout into nine equal sections with the two horizontal and two vertical lines in the banner. Each of these sections will take about 33,33% of the horizontal space and the same percent of the vertical space.
